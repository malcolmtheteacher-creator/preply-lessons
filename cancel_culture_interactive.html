Speaking Practice: Case Studies

Discuss these scenarios with your teacher. Should this person be cancelled? Why or why not?

Scenario 1: The Old Tweet

A 28-year-old YouTuber with 5 million subscribers is discovered to have posted racist jokes on Twitter when they were 16. The tweets were typical "edgy humor" at that time. They have apologized and said they have grown. Brands are dropping sponsorships.

Questions to consider: How old were they? Have they changed? Is the punishment fair?

Scenario 2: The Workplace Allegations

Five former employees anonymously accuse a famous TV host of creating a toxic work environment with bullying. The host denies everything and suggests these are disgruntled workers. No formal investigation has occurred.

Questions to consider: Should we believe anonymous accusers? What about innocent until proven guilty?

Scenario 3: The Political View

A beloved children's book author tweets support for a controversial political position that many consider harmful to marginalized groups. Fans are divided. Some say "separate art from artist," others are boycotting all their books.

Questions to consider: Do people have the right to controversial opinions? When does an opinion cause real harm?

Extended Discussion Questions

Choose 2-3 questions to discuss with your teacher:

The Boundaries:

  • Where is the line between holding someone accountable and cancel culture?
  • Should the severity of cancellation match the severity of the offense?
  • Are some offenses unforgivable? Which ones, and why?

The Process:

  • Does cancel culture skip due process? Should we wait for investigations?
  • What role does social media play in amplifying mob mentality?
  • Is it fair to judge old behavior by today's standards?

Redemption and Change:

  • Is there a path to redemption? What should someone do to be forgiven?
  • How long should someone be cancelled for? Weeks? Years? Forever?
  • Can you think of examples where someone successfully came back after being cancelled?

Power and Impact:

  • Does cancel culture give power to marginalized voices or create a toxic environment?
  • Are celebrities cancelled more easily than regular people? Is this fair?
  • What happens when cancel culture gets it wrong? What about falsely accused people?
