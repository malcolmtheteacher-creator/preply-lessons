Why Are Millions Obsessed with Murder?

True crime is a $1 BILLION industry

Every week, millions of people around the world listen to podcasts about serial killers, binge-watch documentaries about unsolved murders, and read books about the darkest crimes in human history. Why?

Research shows that women make up approximately 70% of true crime audiences. Psychologists suggest that women may be instinctively drawn to these stories as a form of threat assessmentâ€”learning how to recognize danger and avoid becoming victims themselves.

There's something morbidly fascinating about trying to understand what makes someone cross that line from ordinary person to criminal. We want to believe we could spot the warning signs, that we'd see it coming.

True crime taps into our evolutionary need to understand danger. In a safe environment (like listening to a podcast at home), we can explore fear without actual risk. It's the same psychology that makes horror movies popular.

But critics argue that we're glorifying violence by turning real tragedies into entertainment. They point out that real families are grieving while we treat their worst moments as content to consume.

Fans counter that true crime media often helps solve cold cases, brings attention to injustice, and gives victims' families a voice. There's something compelling about seeking justice for people who've been forgotten by the system.

Discussing Ethics & Criticism

Now let's look at how to express opinions about controversial aspects of true crime (or any controversial topic).

4. "We're glorifying..."
Formality: Neutral
Meaning: Making something seem better, more important, or more acceptable than it should be. Often used critically to say we're treating something too positively when we shouldn't.
Example:
"Some people worry we're glorifying violence by turning real murders into entertainment. They think we forget these were real people who suffered."
NOW YOU TRY: Do you think true crime glorifies violence, or does it serve a legitimate purpose? Where's the line?
5. "There's something compelling about..."
