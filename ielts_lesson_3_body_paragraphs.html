Why Body Paragraphs Matter

Body paragraphs are where you score your marks. The introduction and conclusion are important, but the body paragraphs are where you demonstrate your ability to:

  • Develop ideas fully (Task Response)
  • Organize thoughts logically (Coherence & Cohesion)
  • Use sophisticated vocabulary (Lexical Resource)
  • Show complex grammar (Grammatical Range & Accuracy)

The #1 Problem Students Face

‚ö†Ô∏è Underdeveloped Ideas

Most students who get Band 6-6.5 write ideas that are too short and too simple. They state a point but don't explain it fully, provide weak examples, or jump to the next idea too quickly.

What Band 7-8 Body Paragraphs Look Like

  • 5-7 sentences per paragraph (not 2-3!)
  • ONE clear main idea per paragraph
  • Full explanation of the idea
  • Specific example or evidence
  • Links back to the thesis

The TEEL Structure Explained

TEEL (also called PEEL) is a proven formula for writing Band 7-8 body paragraphs. Master this structure and you'll never write weak paragraphs again!

T - Topic Sentence

What it is: The main idea of the paragraph

Purpose: Tell the reader what this paragraph is about

Length: 1 sentence

Example:
"Firstly, online learning provides greater flexibility for students."

E - Explanation

What it is: Develop and explain your topic sentence

Purpose: Show understanding and develop the idea

Length: 2-3 sentences

Example:
"This means students can study at their own pace and schedule classes around other commitments such as work or family responsibilities. Unlike traditional classroom learning, online courses allow learners to access materials 24/7 from anywhere with internet access."

E - Evidence/Example

What it is: Specific example or evidence

Purpose: Support your explanation with concrete details

Length: 1-2 sentences

Example:
"For instance, a working parent might attend lectures in the evening after their children are asleep, while a full-time employee could complete coursework during lunch breaks."

L - Link

What it is: Connect back to thesis or transition

Purpose: Show coherence and wrap up the point

Length: 1 sentence

Example:
"This flexibility therefore makes education accessible to a much wider range of people who might otherwise be unable to pursue their studies."

Complete TEEL Paragraph Example

[TOPIC] Firstly, online learning provides greater flexibility for students. [EXPLAIN] This means students can study at their own pace and schedule classes around other commitments such as work or family responsibilities. Unlike traditional classroom learning, online courses allow learners to access materials 24/7 from anywhere with internet access. [EVIDENCE] For instance, a working parent might attend lectures in the evening after their children are asleep, while a full-time employee could complete coursework during lunch breaks. [LINK] This flexibility therefore makes education accessible to a much wider range of people who might otherwise be unable to pursue their studies.

[Total: 5 sentences, ~90 words - Perfect for Band 7-8!]

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ‚ùå Too short: Only 2-3 sentences per paragraph
  • ‚ùå No explanation: Jumping straight from topic to example
  • ‚ùå Vague examples: Using "people" or "some studies" without specifics
  • ‚ùå No link back: Forgetting to connect to the main argument
  • ‚ùå Multiple ideas: Trying to cover 2-3 ideas in one paragraph

üí° Tips for Building Paragraphs

  • Topic Sentence: Use signposting words (Firstly, Secondly, Furthermore)
  • Explanation: Ask yourself "Why?" and "How?" to extend ideas
  • Evidence: Use "For example," "For instance," "In particular"
  • Link: Use "This shows," "Therefore," "As a result"

How to Extend Ideas to 5-7 Sentences

The key to Band 7-8 is fully developing ideas. Here are proven techniques to extend your paragraphs without repeating yourself.

Technique 1: Ask "Why?" and "How?"

‚ùå Weak (Band 6)

Online learning is convenient for students. They can study from home.

Problem: Only 2 sentences, no development

‚úÖ Strong (Band 7-8)

Online learning is convenient for students. Why? Because they can access course materials from anywhere with an internet connection. How does this help? This eliminates commuting time and allows students to balance education with work or family commitments. What's the result? As a result, more people can pursue higher education who otherwise couldn't due to geographical or time constraints.

Better: Extended to 4 sentences with full development

Technique 2: Add Specific Details

‚ùå Vague Example

For example, some people use online learning to get better jobs.

‚úÖ Specific Example

For instance, a working nurse in Australia might enroll in an online Master's degree program at a UK university, attending live lectures at 8 PM her time while completing assignments on weekends. This flexibility allowed her to advance her career to a management position without taking a career break.

Technique 3: Use Contrasts

Example:

"Online learning provides flexibility. Unlike traditional universities where students must attend lectures at fixed times, online courses allow learners to watch recorded sessions at their convenience. In contrast to face-to-face education which requires physical presence, digital learning only needs an internet connection."

Contrasting with traditional methods adds depth and shows critical thinking!

Technique 4: Explain Consequences

Example:

"Online learning increases accessibility. This means that students in remote areas can access quality education. As a result, educational inequality between urban and rural areas decreases. The long-term effect is a more educated population regardless of geographical location."

Evidence vs. Explanation

Many students confuse explanation with evidence. Click on each card to identify whether it's EXPLANATION or EVIDENCE.

Remember:

  • Explanation: Develops the idea, answers "why" and "how"
  • Evidence: Specific example, statistic, or case study

Practice: Identify Explanation vs. Evidence

EXPLANATION

"This flexibility allows students to manage their time more effectively and balance multiple responsibilities."

EVIDENCE

"For instance, a 2023 Harvard study found that 67% of online learners were able to complete their degrees while working full-time jobs."

EVIDENCE

"In Singapore, the government introduced online learning platforms during the pandemic, and over 500,000 students successfully completed courses from home."

EXPLANATION

"When companies invest in employee training, productivity increases because workers feel valued and gain new skills that improve their performance."

EVIDENCE

"For example, Google provides 20% time for employees to work on passion projects, which led to innovations like Gmail and Google News."

EXPLANATION

"This approach benefits both individuals and society by creating a more skilled workforce and reducing unemployment rates."

Transform Weak Paragraphs into Band 7-8

Learn to identify problems in weak paragraphs and fix them using TEEL structure and extension techniques.

Example 1: Too Short (Band 5-6)

‚ùå Weak Paragraph:

"Firstly, technology helps education. Students can learn online. This is good for people who live far from schools."

Problems:

  • Only 3 sentences (too short!)
  • No explanation of HOW technology helps
  • No specific example
  • Vague language ("good," "helps")

‚úÖ Improved Paragraph (Band 7-8):

[T] Firstly, technology significantly enhances educational access for remote learners. [E] This is because digital platforms enable students to participate in live classes, access recorded lectures, and submit assignments without needing to physically attend a campus. In regions where quality schools are scarce, online education effectively bridges the geographical gap. [E] For instance, in rural Australia, students in the outback use satellite internet to attend virtual classes at prestigious urban schools, receiving the same quality education as their city counterparts. [L] This technological advancement therefore ensures that location no longer limits educational opportunities.

Improvements:

  • ‚úÖ Extended to 5 sentences
  • ‚úÖ Full TEEL structure
  • ‚úÖ Specific example (rural Australia)
  • ‚úÖ Sophisticated vocabulary

Example 2: No Development (Band 6)

‚ùå Weak Paragraph:

"Secondly, exercise improves health. People who exercise live longer. For example, running is good exercise. Therefore, everyone should exercise regularly."

Problems:

  • No real explanation of HOW or WHY
  • Vague example (just says "running")
  • Obvious statements without depth
  • No critical thinking

‚úÖ Improved Paragraph (Band 7-8):

[T] Secondly, regular physical activity significantly reduces the risk of chronic diseases and extends life expectancy. [E] This occurs because exercise strengthens cardiovascular function, regulates blood pressure, and helps maintain healthy body weight, all of which are crucial factors in preventing conditions such as heart disease, diabetes, and certain cancers. Moreover, physical activity stimulates the release of endorphins, which improve mental health and reduce stress-related illnesses. [E] A compelling example is the Copenhagen City Heart Study, which tracked 20,000 adults over 35 years and found that joggers lived an average of 6.2 years longer than non-joggers, even when controlling for other health factors. [L] This evidence strongly suggests that incorporating regular exercise into daily routines is one of the most effective ways to improve both lifespan and quality of life.
