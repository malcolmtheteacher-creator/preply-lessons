Tim Cook - CEO of Apple

Job: He runs Apple (one of the biggest companies in the world)
Wake up time: 3:45 AM (Yes, really!)

His Morning Routine:
  1. 3:45 AM - Wake up (no alarm clock needed!)
  2. 4:00 AM - Read emails and drink coffee
  3. 5:00 AM - Go to the gym for one hour
  4. 6:30 AM - Shower and eat a simple breakfast
  5. 7:00 AM - Start work (he's the first person in the office!)
Why he does this: "I get more work done before 9 AM than most people do all day. The morning is quiet. Nobody bothers me. I can think clearly."

Oprah Winfrey - TV Host and Businesswoman

Job: Famous TV personality and entrepreneur
Wake up time: 6:00 AM

Her Morning Routine:
  1. 6:00 AM - Wake up naturally (no alarm)
  2. 6:15 AM - Meditation for 20 minutes (sitting quietly, thinking about life)
  3. 6:45 AM - Walk her dogs outside
  4. 7:15 AM - Exercise (yoga or running)
  5. 8:00 AM - Healthy breakfast (fruit, yogurt, tea)
  6. 8:30 AM - Read news and start work
Why she does this: "I don't check my phone when I wake up. The first hour of the day is MINE. I take care of myself first, then I take care of work."

Mark Wahlberg - Actor and Businessman

Job: Hollywood actor (movies like Transformers, Ted)
Wake up time: 2:30 AM (The earliest!)

His Morning Routine:
  1. 2:30 AM - Wake up
  2. 2:45 AM - Pray and meditate
  3. 3:15 AM - First workout at the gym
  4. 5:00 AM - Eat first breakfast (yes, he eats TWO breakfasts!)
  5. 5:30 AM - Second workout
  6. 7:30 AM - Second breakfast
  7. 8:00 AM - Golf (for fun and business meetings)
Why he does this: "While everyone else is sleeping, I'm getting better. I work out twice because I want to stay strong and healthy. Morning is my power time."

Talking About Daily Habits

How to use these expressions:
Click each card to see the meaning and examples. These are simple, natural expressions that English speakers use every day to talk about their routines.
1. "I usually wake up at..."
Meaning: This is my normal wake-up time (most days)
Grammar: I/You/We/They + usually + verb
When to use: Talking about your regular routine
Examples:
‚Ä¢ "I usually wake up at 7:00 AM on weekdays."
‚Ä¢ "She usually wakes up early because she likes mornings."
‚Ä¢ "We usually wake up late on Sundays."
Vocabulary Help:
‚Ä¢ usually = most of the time (not always, but almost always)
‚Ä¢ wake up = open your eyes in the morning, stop sleeping
2. "The first thing I do is..."
Meaning: This is the very first action in my morning
When to use: Describing the start of your routine
Examples:
‚Ä¢ "The first thing I do is check my phone." (common!)
‚Ä¢ "The first thing I do is drink water."
‚Ä¢ "The first thing Tim Cook does is read his emails."
Vocabulary Help:
‚Ä¢ first thing = before anything else
‚Ä¢ check = look at something quickly
3. "I never skip..."
Meaning: I always do this, I don't miss it
Grammar: never + verb = 0% (you don't do it)
When to use: Talking about important habits
Examples:
‚Ä¢ "I never skip breakfast. It's the most important meal!"
‚Ä¢ "She never skips her morning exercise."
‚Ä¢ "Oprah never skips her meditation time."
Vocabulary Help:
‚Ä¢ skip = not do something (miss it)
‚Ä¢ meal = breakfast, lunch, or dinner
4. "It takes me ... to..."
Meaning: I need this much time to complete this action
Grammar: It takes + person + time + to + verb
When to use: Talking about how long something needs
Examples:
‚Ä¢ "It takes me 30 minutes to get ready in the morning."
‚Ä¢ "It takes Tim Cook one hour to exercise."
‚Ä¢ "It takes her 15 minutes to eat breakfast."
Vocabulary Help:
‚Ä¢ get ready = prepare yourself (shower, dress, etc.)
‚Ä¢ takes = needs (time)
5. "I try to..."
Meaning: I make an effort to do this (but sometimes I can't)
When to use: Talking about goals or things you want to do regularly
Examples:
‚Ä¢ "I try to exercise three times a week."
‚Ä¢ "I try to go to bed before midnight."
‚Ä¢ "She tries to eat healthy food for breakfast."
Vocabulary Help:
‚Ä¢ try to = make an effort, attempt
‚Ä¢ go to bed = lie down to sleep
6. "Before I..., I always..."
Meaning: I do the second action before the first action (order is important!)
When to use: Showing the sequence of your routine
Examples:
‚Ä¢ "Before I leave home, I always check I have my keys."
‚Ä¢ "Before I eat breakfast, I always brush my teeth."
‚Ä¢ "Before Oprah starts work, she always walks her dogs."
Vocabulary Help:
‚Ä¢ before = earlier than
‚Ä¢ leave = go away from a place

Talking About Healthy Routines

Remember: All three successful people (Tim Cook, Oprah, Mark Wahlberg) exercise in the morning and eat healthy food. Let's learn how to talk about healthy habits!
7. "I make time for..."
Meaning: I create space in my schedule for this activity (it's important to me)
When to use: Showing priorities, things you value
Examples:
‚Ä¢ "I make time for exercise every morning."
‚Ä¢ "She makes time for meditation before work."
‚Ä¢ "Tim Cook makes time for the gym at 5:00 AM."
Vocabulary Help:
‚Ä¢ make time = find time in your schedule
‚Ä¢ meditation = sitting quietly and thinking/relaxing
8. "I'm not a morning person"
Meaning: I don't like mornings, I prefer to wake up late
Opposite: "I'm a morning person" = I love mornings!
When to use: Explaining your energy levels at different times
Examples:
‚Ä¢ "I'm not a morning person. I feel tired until 10 AM."
‚Ä¢ "She's definitely a morning person - she wakes up at 5 AM feeling great!"
‚Ä¢ "Are you a morning person or a night person?"
Vocabulary Help:
‚Ä¢ morning person = someone who has energy in the morning
‚Ä¢ night person = someone who has energy at night
9. "I work out..."
Meaning: I exercise (phrasal verb - very common!)
Same as: exercise, go to the gym, train
When to use: Talking about physical exercise
Examples:
‚Ä¢ "I work out three times a week at the gym."
‚Ä¢ "Mark Wahlberg works out twice every morning!"
‚Ä¢ "She works out at home because the gym is expensive."
Vocabulary Help:
‚Ä¢ work out = exercise (phrasal verb from your last lesson!)
‚Ä¢ gym = fitness center with equipment
10. "I feel more... when I..."
Meaning: This action makes me feel this way
When to use: Explaining the benefits of your habits
Examples:
‚Ä¢ "I feel more energetic when I exercise in the morning."
‚Ä¢ "I feel more productive when I wake up early."
‚Ä¢ "Oprah feels more calm when she meditates."
Vocabulary Help:
‚Ä¢ energetic = full of energy
‚Ä¢ productive = you get a lot done
‚Ä¢ calm = relaxed, not stressed
11. "I can't start my day without..."
Meaning: This is essential for me, I need it every morning
When to use: Talking about your most important morning habits
Examples:
‚Ä¢ "I can't start my day without coffee!" (very common!)
‚Ä¢ "I can't start my day without a shower."
‚Ä¢ "Tim Cook can't start his day without reading emails."
Vocabulary Help:
‚Ä¢ can't = cannot (impossible)
‚Ä¢ without = if I don't have this
12. "I've gotten into the habit of..."
Meaning: I started doing this regularly, now it's normal for me
When to use: Talking about new routines you developed
Examples:
‚Ä¢ "I've gotten into the habit of drinking water when I wake up."
‚Ä¢ "She's gotten into the habit of walking to work."
‚Ä¢ "He's gotten into the habit of reading for 30 minutes every morning."
Vocabulary Help:
‚Ä¢ gotten into the habit = made something a regular routine
‚Ä¢ habit = something you do regularly without thinking
