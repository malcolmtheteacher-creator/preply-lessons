Expressing Concern & Skepticism (Advanced Level)

Click each expression to see meaning and real-world example
1. "I'm concerned that..." / "My concern is that..."
Meaning: Express worry about a specific consequence
Level: Formal/Professional
"I'm concerned that we're moving too fast without considering social impact. My concern is that millions will be unemployed before we've figured out how to support them."
2. "I'm skeptical about..." / "I'm somewhat skeptical that..."
Meaning: You doubt something is true or will work as claimed
Level: Neutral/Intellectual
"Companies say AI will 'create new jobs,' but I'm skeptical about that. I'm somewhat skeptical that retraining a 50-year-old truck driver to become a data scientist is realistic."
3. "What worries me most is..." / "My biggest worry is..."
Meaning: Highlight your main fear from multiple possibilities
Level: Neutral
"People talk about job loss, bias, privacy... but what worries me most is that we're giving up human judgment. My biggest worry is that we'll start trusting algorithms over our own thinking."
4. "I find it hard to believe that..." / "I struggle to accept that..."
Meaning: Polite way to say "I don't believe this"
Level: Formal/Diplomatic
"Tech CEOs say AI will give us more free time. I find it hard to believe that, given that every 'productivity tool' of the past 30 years just made us work more."
5. "There's a real risk that..." / "We run the risk of..."
Meaning: Warn about a genuine danger
Level: Formal/Professional
"Everyone's excited about AI tutors, but there's a real risk we're replacing human connection with screens. We run the risk of raising a generation that can't relate to actual people."
6. "I can't help but feel that..." / "I can't shake the feeling that..."
Meaning: Express an intuition you can't ignore
Level: Neutral/Personal
"I can't help but feel that we're sleepwalking into something dangerous. I can't shake the feeling that in 10 years, we'll say 'We should have slowed down.'"

    7. "It's likely that..." / "It's highly likely that..."
    Meaning: You think this will probably happen (70-90% chance)
    Level: Formal/Neutral
    "It's likely that within 5 years, most customer service jobs will be automated. It's highly likely that entry-level jobs in law and medicine will be significantly reduced."
    8. "I wouldn't be surprised if..." / "It wouldn't shock me if..."
    Meaning: Something is quite possible, though not certain
    Level: Neutral/Informal
    "I wouldn't be surprised if universities start losing students because people can just ask AI. It wouldn't shock me if in 10 years, a 4-year degree seems completely outdated."
    9. "In all likelihood..." / "The chances are..."
    Meaning: Making an educated guess based on evidence
    Level: Formal
    "In all likelihood, AI will get exponentially better over the next decade. The chances are that what seems impossible today will be routine in 10 years."
    10. "One possible scenario is..." / "Here's what might happen..."
    Meaning: Present one potential future (acknowledging others)
    Level: Neutral
    "One possible scenario is we end up with two classes: people who work WITH AI and people who don't. Here's what might happenâ€”the AI-literate will thrive, everyone else struggles."
    11. "If current trends continue..." / "If things keep going this way..."
    Meaning: Prediction based on current trajectory
    Level: Neutral
    "If current trends continue, by 2030 we'll have AI that can do any intellectual work. If things keep going this way, the question won't be 'Can AI do my job?' but 'Why hire a human?'"
    12. "I imagine that..." / "I'd imagine..." / "I suspect that..."
    Meaning: Personal speculation, slightly less certain than "likely"
    Level: Neutral
    "I imagine most workers will have AI assistants within 3 years. I suspect that 'AI skills' will be as essential as computer literacy is today."
