Key Vocabulary

Click on each card to reveal the definition and example. Then discuss how YOU would use this word!

Digital Footprint

Meaning: The trail of data you leave behind when you use the internet - posts, comments, likes, searches, etc.

Example: "Be careful what you post - your digital footprint can affect job applications."

Misinformation

Meaning: False or inaccurate information spread unintentionally (compare: disinformation = spread intentionally to deceive).

Example: "A lot of misinformation about vaccines spread on social media during COVID."

Echo Chamber

Meaning: A situation where you only see information that reinforces your existing beliefs (because algorithms show you similar content).

Example: "Social media creates echo chambers where people never hear opposing viewpoints."

Doomscrolling

Meaning: Continuously scrolling through negative news on social media, even though it makes you feel anxious or sad.

Example: "I was doomscrolling through disaster news at 2 AM and couldn't sleep."

Clickbait

Meaning: Sensationalized headlines designed to make you click, often misleading.

Example: "That headline 'You won't believe what happened next!' is obvious clickbait."

Influencer

Meaning: A person with a large social media following who can affect the opinions or purchasing decisions of their audience.

Example: "Many influencers get paid thousands of dollars per sponsored post."

Curated Feed

Meaning: A carefully selected and arranged collection of posts that show only the "best" or "perfect" moments of someone's life.

Example: "Her Instagram looks perfect, but it's a curated feed - real life isn't like that."

Data Privacy

Meaning: The right to control how your personal information is collected and used by companies and websites.

Example: "Facebook faced criticism for poor data privacy practices."

Viral

Meaning: When content spreads rapidly across the internet as people share it.

Example: "That video went viral - it got 50 million views in two days!"

Trolling

Meaning: Deliberately posting controversial or offensive comments online to provoke reactions or upset people.

Example: "Don't engage with trolls - they just want attention."

True, False, or "It Depends"?

For each statement, decide if it's TRUE, FALSE, or IT DEPENDS. Then discuss WHY with your teacher!

Statement 1: If you delete a post from social media, it's gone forever.

Discuss: Why did you choose this answer? What happens to deleted content?

Statement 2: Social media companies have the right to remove any content they don't like.

Discuss: Should companies be able to censor content? Where's the line?

Statement 3: Influencers should be held responsible if they promote a product that turns out to be harmful or fake.

Discuss: Are influencers just advertisers? Or do they have extra responsibility?

Statement 4: Reading news on social media is just as reliable as reading traditional newspapers.

Discuss: Where do you get your news? How do you know if it's trustworthy?

Statement 5: Children under 13 should be banned from using social media.

Discuss: At what age should kids be allowed on social media? Why?

Statement 6: Using filters and editing your photos before posting is dishonest.

Discuss: Where's the line between "touching up" a photo and creating a fake image?

Scenario 1: The Family Intervention

üë®‚Äçüëß Character A: The Parent

Your concern: Your 14-year-old daughter spends 6 hours daily on TikTok. Her grades are dropping and she seems depressed. You want to limit her phone time.

Your goal: Convince her that social media is harming her, but don't want to be too controlling.

üëß Character B: The Teenager

  • Who had the stronger argument?
  • What would be a fair solution?
  • Is 6 hours too much? How much is reasonable?
