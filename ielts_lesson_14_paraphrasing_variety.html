Why Paraphrasing Matters in IELTS

Critical for Lexical Resource Score

Paraphrasing demonstrates your vocabulary range and is essential for Band 7+ in Lexical Resource. Examiners specifically look for your ability to express the same ideas using different words.

Where You MUST Paraphrase:

  1. Introduction: Restate the question in your own words
  2. Throughout Essay: Refer back to main ideas using different vocabulary
  3. Conclusion: Summarize without repeating exact phrases from introduction
  4. Topic Sentences: Reference the question without copying it

The Impact on Your Score

Band 6 (Too Much Repetition)

Question: "Should teenagers do community service?"

Introduction: "Many people think teenagers should do community service. I agree that teenagers should do community service."

Problem: Repeats "teenagers should do community service" exactly

Band 7-8 (Good Paraphrasing)

Question: "Should teenagers do community service?"

Introduction: "Many people believe young people should engage in voluntary work. I firmly agree that adolescents benefit from participating in unpaid community programs."

Success: Uses different words while maintaining meaning

Key Principle:

Paraphrasing is NOT about using fancy words - it's about showing you can express the same idea in multiple natural ways. Simple, accurate paraphrasing is better than complex, awkward paraphrasing.

Four Essential Paraphrasing Techniques

Technique 1: Synonym Substitution

Replace words with synonyms (same meaning, different word)

Original Word Synonyms Example
important crucial, vital, essential, significant Education is important > Education is crucial
children young people, youth, adolescents, youngsters Children need exercise > Young people need exercise
increase rise, grow, expand, escalate Prices increase > Prices rise
solve address, tackle, resolve, deal with Solve the problem > Address the issue
advantages benefits, merits, positive aspects The advantages are clear > The benefits are evident
Warning: Not all synonyms work in all contexts. "Big" and "large" are synonyms, but "big problem" sounds more natural than "large problem."

Technique 2: Word Form Changes

Change the grammatical form of words (noun > verb > adjective)

Example 1:
Original: "Education is important" (noun)
→ Paraphrase: "It is important to educate young people" (verb)
Example 2:
Original: "The government should act" (verb)
→ Paraphrase: "Government action is necessary" (noun)
Example 3:
Original: "People who are poor" (adjective)
→ Paraphrase: "Those living in poverty" (noun)
Example 4:
Original: "Technology develops rapidly" (adverb)
→ Paraphrase: "The rapid development of technology" (adjective)

Technique 3: Sentence Structure Changes

Reorganize the sentence structure while keeping the meaning

Active to Passive:
Original: "The government should regulate social media"
→ Paraphrase: "Social media should be regulated by authorities"
Change Word Order:
Original: "Climate change is one of the biggest threats"
→ Paraphrase: "One of the most significant threats we face is climate change"
Split/Combine Sentences:
Original: "Technology has improved communication but reduced face-to-face interaction"
→ Paraphrase: "Technology has enhanced how we communicate. However, it has decreased personal interactions."

Technique 4: Use of Different Phrases

Replace phrases with equivalent expressions

Original Phrases:
  • in my opinion
  • nowadays
  • more and more
  • a lot of
  • because of
  • in order to
Alternative Phrases:
  • I believe that / from my perspective
  • in modern society / currently
  • increasingly / a growing number of
  • numerous / many / substantial
  • due to / owing to / as a result of
  • to / so as to / with the aim of

Golden Rule:

Combine 2-3 techniques for best results. Don't just change one word - use synonyms + structure changes + word form changes together.

Original IELTS Question:

Some people believe that teenagers should be required to do unpaid community service in their free time. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

Your Paraphrased Version:

Example Paraphrases:

Strong Paraphrase:

"Many argue that young people should engage in voluntary work during their leisure time. This essay will discuss the extent to which I support mandatory community programs for adolescents."

Why it works: Changes "teenagers" to "young people/adolescents", "unpaid community service" to "voluntary work/community programs", "free time" to "leisure time"

Weak Paraphrase (Too Much Repetition):

"Some people think teenagers should do unpaid community service in their free time. I agree that teenagers should do community service."

Why it fails: Repeats "teenagers," "unpaid community service," and "free time" exactly from the question

Sentence Transformation Practice

Rewrite these sentences using different structures while maintaining the same meaning.

1. "The government should invest more money in public transportation."

Your paraphrase:

Hint: Try changing "invest" to "investment" (noun form) or use passive voice "should be invested"

Example Paraphrases:

  • Greater investment in public transport by authorities is necessary.
  • More funding should be allocated to public transport systems.
  • It is essential for the government to dedicate additional resources to public transportation.

2. "Many young people spend too much time on social media."

Your paraphrase:

Hint: Start with "Excessive" or "An increasing number of" and change "young people" to "adolescents" or "youth"

Example Paraphrases:

  • An excessive amount of time is devoted to social networking sites by adolescents.
  • Excessive social media usage is common among today's youth.
  • A significant proportion of teenagers dedicate too many hours to online platforms.

3. "Climate change is causing serious environmental problems."

Your paraphrase:

Hint: Change "causing" to "cause" (noun) or start with "Serious environmental problems"

Example Paraphrases:

  • Significant ecological issues are arising due to global warming.
  • Serious environmental problems result from climate change.
  • The changing climate is leading to severe ecological damage.

Avoiding Repetition Throughout Your Essay

The Problem:

Repeating the same words and phrases throughout your essay makes it boring and suggests limited vocabulary. Band 7+ essays show lexical variety - the ability to express ideas in multiple ways.

Example: Essay with Too Much Repetition (Band 6)

Some people believe that technology has improved our lives. I agree that technology is beneficial because technology helps us communicate better and technology makes work easier. Firstly, technology allows us to stay connected. For example, technology like smartphones helps people talk to family. Furthermore, technology improves productivity at work.

Problem: The word "technology" appears 8 times in just a few sentences!

Improved Version with Lexical Variety (Band 7-8)

Some people believe that technological advances have improved our lives. I agree that modern innovations are beneficial because they help us communicate better and make work easier. Firstly, digital tools allow us to stay connected. For example, devices like smartphones help people talk to family. Furthermore, these innovations improve productivity at work.

Better: Uses "technological advances," "modern innovations," "digital tools," "devices," "these innovations," and pronouns like "they"

Strategies for Variety

Strategy 1: Use Pronouns and Reference Words

Instead of Repeating Use Example
technology, technology it, this, these advances Technology helps communication. It also improves education.
students, students they, learners, young people Students benefit from exercise. They become healthier.
the problem, the problem this issue, such challenges Pollution is serious. This issue affects everyone.

Strategy 2: Create a Vocabulary Chain

Plan alternative words before writing:

Topic: Education
Main word: education > schooling > academic learning > formal instruction > the educational system
Use different versions throughout your essay
Topic: Environment
Main word: environment > natural world > ecosystem > our planet > the ecological system
Rotate these terms in different paragraphs
Topic: Technology
Main word: technology > technological advances > digital innovations > modern devices > technical developments
Keep variety across body paragraphs

Strategy 3: When Repetition IS Acceptable

You CAN repeat words in these situations:

  • Technical terms with no good synonym (e.g., "photosynthesis," "democracy")
  • Question keywords occasionally (but paraphrase them most of the time)
  • Common words like "people," "society," "government" - but still vary when possible
  • Different paragraphs - if you use "technology" in paragraph 2, you can use it again in paragraph 4

Golden Rule:

Don't use the same noun or verb more than twice in one paragraph. If you find yourself writing a word for the third time, STOP and use a synonym or pronoun instead.

Common Paraphrasing Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Changing Only One Word

Question: "Some people think university education should be free."

Weak paraphrase: "Some people individuals think university education should be free."

Why it fails: Only changed "people" to "individuals" - everything else is identical

Better:

"Many argue that higher education should be provided without charge."

Why it works: Changed multiple elements: "some people" > "many," "think" > "argue," "university education" > "higher education," "free" > "without charge"

Mistake 2: Using Unnatural or Incorrect Synonyms

Original: "Children should exercise regularly."

Wrong: "Offspring should exercise regularly."

Why it fails: "Offspring" is technically correct but sounds very unnatural and overly formal

Better:

"Young people should engage in physical activity regularly."

Why it works: Natural alternatives: "children" > "young people," "exercise" > "engage in physical activity"

Mistake 3: Changing the Meaning

Original: "Working from home has increased during the pandemic."

Wrong: "Remote work has stopped during the pandemic."

Why it fails: Completely changed the meaning - "increased" vs "stopped" are opposites!

Better:

"Remote working has become more common due to COVID-19."

Why it works: Maintains meaning: "working from home" > "remote working," "increased" > "become more common," "pandemic" > "COVID-19"

Mistake 4: Copying Too Much from the Question

Question: "Some people believe that teenagers should be required to do unpaid community service."

Weak introduction: "Some people believe that teenagers should be required to do unpaid community service. I agree with this."

Why it fails: Copied the entire question word-for-word, then added one sentence

Better:

"It is argued that adolescents should participate in mandatory volunteer work. I firmly agree that young people benefit from engaging in community programs."

Why it works: Paraphrased every key element and extended with own words

Mistake 5: Over-Paraphrasing Simple Words

Original: "People use smartphones every day."

Awkward: "Homo sapiens utilize telecommunications devices on a daily basis."

Why it fails: Unnecessarily complex and unnatural - trying too hard to sound academic

Better:

"Individuals rely on mobile devices daily."

Why it works: Natural paraphrasing: "people" > "individuals," "use" > "rely on," "smartphones" > "mobile devices"

Key Takeaway:

Paraphrasing should sound natural. If your paraphrase sounds awkward or overly complex, you've gone too far. Aim for clarity and naturalness, not fancy vocabulary.

Complete Practice Exercise

Paraphrase this complete IELTS introduction. Use multiple techniques!

IELTS Question:

"In many countries, the quality of life in large cities is becoming worse. What are the causes of this problem? What measures can be taken to solve it?"

Task: Write a complete introduction paraphrasing this question

Your introduction should:

  1. Paraphrase the background statement
  2. Paraphrase both questions
  3. Add your thesis/roadmap
  4. Total: 3-4 sentences

Model Answer:

Introduction (Band 8):

"The standard of living in major urban centers worldwide has been declining in recent years. This essay will examine the primary factors contributing to this deterioration and propose effective solutions to address these challenges. The main causes include overcrowding and pollution, while potential remedies involve improved urban planning and stricter environmental regulations."

Analysis of Paraphrasing:

  • "quality of life" > "standard of living"
  • "large cities" > "major urban centers"
  • "becoming worse" > "has been declining" / "deterioration"
  • "causes of this problem" > "factors contributing" / "main causes"
  • "measures can be taken" > "propose effective solutions" / "potential remedies"
  • "solve it" > "address these challenges"

Alternative Model Answer (Band 7-8):

"Living conditions in metropolitan areas across the globe are experiencing a notable decline. This essay will explore the root causes behind this trend and suggest practical measures to tackle this issue. Overcrowding and environmental degradation appear to be the primary culprits, and the solutions may lie in sustainable urban development and tighter pollution controls."

Checklist for Your Introduction:

  • Did you paraphrase EVERY key word from the question?
  • Does it still have the same meaning as the original?
  • Does it sound natural (not forced or overly complex)?
  • Did you use 2-3 different paraphrasing techniques?
  • Did you add your thesis/roadmap?
  • Is it 3-4 sentences long?
