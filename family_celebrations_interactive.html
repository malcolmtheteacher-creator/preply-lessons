Essential Vocabulary üìö

üìå Instructions:

Click on each vocabulary card to see the definition and example. Try to use these words when speaking today!

tradition (noun)
Definition: A custom or way of doing something that has been passed down through families or cultures for many years.
üí¨ "In my family, we have a tradition of eating together every Sunday."
gather (verb)
Definition: To come together in one place, usually for a special reason.
üí¨ "All my relatives gathered at my grandparents' house for the festival."
ceremony (noun)
Definition: A formal event with special actions, often for important occasions like weddings or graduations.
üí¨ "The wedding ceremony was beautiful and lasted about two hours."
host / throw a party (verb phrases)
Definition: To organize and invite people to a celebration or event.
üí¨ "My sister hosted a big party for her 30th birthday."
üí¨ "We threw a surprise party for my dad last year."
decorate (verb)
Definition: To make a place look beautiful or festive by adding special items like flowers, lights, or balloons.
üí¨ "We decorated the house with colorful lights and flowers."
memorable (adjective)
Definition: Something that you will remember for a long time because it was special or important.
üí¨ "My brother's wedding was the most memorable event of last year."
honor / honour (verb)
Definition: To show respect or give special attention to someone or something important.
üí¨ "We honor our grandparents by visiting them on special days."
feast (noun)
Definition: A large special meal with lots of food, usually for a celebration.
üí¨ "We prepared a huge feast with many traditional dishes."
exchange gifts (verb phrase)
Definition: To give presents to each other.
üí¨ "On birthdays, we always exchange gifts with close family members."
mark the occasion (phrase)
Definition: To do something special to celebrate or remember an important event.
üí¨ "We marked the occasion by taking photos and having a special dinner."

Tips for Timed Speaking üí°

  • Don't worry about making small mistakes - keep talking!
  • If you can't think of the perfect word, describe it another way
  • Use connecting words: "and", "but", "because", "so", "also", "then"
  • Add details: colors, numbers, feelings, comparisons
  • If you finish early, add more examples or describe more details

Role Play Scenarios üé≠

üìå Instructions:

Choose a role play below. Read your role, then have a natural conversation with your teacher. Try to keep the conversation going for 3-5 minutes!

Scenario 1: Planning a Surprise Birthday Party üéÇ

üë§ Your Role: The Party Planner

You want to organize a surprise birthday party for your friend's 30th birthday. Talk to your friend's partner (teacher) about:

  • When and where to have the party
  • Who to invite
  • What food and drinks to prepare
  • How to keep it a surprise
  • Budget and who will pay for what
  • Decorations and entertainment

Scenario 2: Wedding Guest and Host üíç

üë§ Your Role: Wedding Guest

You're invited to a wedding in a different culture. Call the host (teacher) to ask about:

  • What you should wear (dress code)
  • Whether you should bring a gift (and what kind)
  • What time to arrive
  • If you can bring your children
  • Special customs or traditions you should know about
  • Dietary options at the reception

Scenario 3: Explaining Your Tradition üéä

üë§ Your Role: Cultural Ambassador

Your foreign friend (teacher) wants to learn about an important celebration in your culture. Explain:

  • The history and meaning of the celebration
  • When and how long it lasts
  • Traditional activities and customs
  • Special food and drinks
  • What people wear
  • Why it's important to your family/culture

Scenario 4: Celebrating Abroad üåç

üë§ Your Role: Person Living Abroad

You're living in a foreign country and want to celebrate an important festival from your home country. Talk to your neighbor (teacher) about:

  • What celebration you want to organize
  • Inviting them to join
  • Explaining what will happen
  • What they can expect (food, activities, duration)
  • Whether they need to bring anything
  • Answering their questions about your tradition

Role Play Tips üé¨
