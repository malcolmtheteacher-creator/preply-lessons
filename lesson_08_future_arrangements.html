üîç Grammar Discovery: Present Continuous for Future

üì± Story Time: Sarah's Week

It's Monday morning. Sarah is talking to her friend:

"I'm really busy this week! Tonight, I'm having dinner with Tom at 7 PM. Tomorrow, I'm meeting my study group at the library. On Wednesday, I'm going to the dentist at 2:30. And this weekend? I'm flying to London to visit my sister! Oh, and we are celebrating Mom's birthday on Saturday evening."

‚ùì Notice something interesting?

  • Is Sarah talking about NOW or the FUTURE?
  • Are these plans or spontaneous decisions?
  • Does she have specific times/days?

üìä The Rule:

USE PRESENT CONTINUOUS FOR FUTURE when:

  • ‚úÖ You have a fixed arrangement (confirmed plan)
  • ‚úÖ There's a specific time or date
  • ‚úÖ You've organized it with someone else
  • ‚úÖ It's in your calendar/schedule

üîÑ Compare the Three Future Forms:

Form Use Example
WILL Spontaneous decision / Prediction "I'll help you!" (just decided)
"It will rain." (prediction)
GOING TO Plans / Intentions (no specific time) "I'm going to study medicine."
(my plan, but when? not fixed yet)
PRESENT CONTINUOUS Fixed arrangements (specific time) "I'm meeting John at 3 PM tomorrow."
(confirmed, specific time, organized)

üî¢ How to Form:

PRESENT CONTINUOUS (for future):

Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing + time expression

  • I am meeting her tomorrow.
  • She is visiting us on Monday.
  • They are flying to Paris tonight.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: You need a future time word (tomorrow, tonight, next week, etc.)

3. You bought flight tickets. Your flight leaves tomorrow at 6 PM.

üí¨ Useful Phrases:

Accepting:

  • "Great! I'm coming to the party on Saturday!"
  • "Perfect! I'm meeting you at 3 PM."
  • "Yes! I'm definitely going to be there!"

Checking your schedule:

  • "Let me check... Am I doing anything on Friday?"
  • "What am I doing on Saturday? Oh, I'm free!"
  • "Wait, I need to check my calendar..."

Declining (politely):

  • "I'm sorry, I'm seeing my family that evening."
  • "I'd love to, but I'm working that day."
  • "Unfortunately, I'm traveling that weekend."

‚úÖ Lesson Review & Summary

üìù What We Learned Today

üéØ Grammar Summary:

PRESENT CONTINUOUS for Future Arrangements

Structure: Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing + time expression

Use for:

  • Fixed arrangements with specific times
  • Confirmed plans with other people
  • Scheduled events (appointments, meetings)
  • Things in your calendar

‚ö†Ô∏è Remember: You MUST include a future time word!

üîÑ Quick Review: Three Future Forms

Situation Which Form?
"I have a dentist appointment at 2 PM tomorrow" Present Continuous: I'm seeing the dentist at 2 PM.
"I decided I want to learn Spanish" (no specific date) Going to: I'm going to learn Spanish.
"The phone is ringing!" (spontaneous offer) Will: I'll answer it!
"I bought tickets. The concert is Saturday at 8 PM." Present Continuous: I'm going to the concert at 8 PM.
"General prediction about 2050" Will: Cars will fly in 2050.

üìö Key Vocabulary Review:

arrangement
A plan that you organize with someone else; something you schedule
"We've made arrangements to meet at 7 PM."
appointment
A formal meeting or arrangement at a specific time
"I have a doctor's appointment on Thursday."
schedule
A plan showing when things will happen; your calendar
"What's on your schedule this week?"
confirm
To make certain that something is definite
"Can you confirm our meeting time?"
available
Free; not busy; able to do something
"Are you available on Friday evening?"
pick someone up
To collect someone from a place (by car, etc.)
"I'm picking you up at 6 PM."
check my calendar
To look at your schedule to see if you're free
"Let me check my calendar... Yes, I'm free!"
be booked
To be reserved or scheduled; busy with plans
"Sorry, I'm fully booked this weekend."
