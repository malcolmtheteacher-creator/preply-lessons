Why Two Different Past Tenses?

English uses two past tenses to show the difference between actions:

üìç Past Simple

Complete actions

‚úì Finished
‚úì Has a clear end
‚úì The main event

(finished action)

üåä Past Continuous

Actions in progress

‚úì Was happening
‚úì Background scene
‚úì Unfinished at that moment

(in progress)

When We Use Them Together:

üåä Background: I was sleeping (in progress, setting the scene)

Interrupted Actions ‚ö°

The Classic Pattern:

I was doing X (in progress) when Y happened (interruption)

Real-Life Examples:

  • I was watching Netflix when the power went out.
  • We were having dinner when someone knocked on the door.
  • I was walking home when I saw my ex-girlfriend.
  • She was texting her friend when she fell down the stairs.

Common Interruption Situations:

Tell Me About a Time When...

1 Your phone rang at a bad moment

What were you doing when it rang? Who was calling? What happened?

2 Someone interrupted you during an important moment

What were you doing? Who interrupted? How did you feel?

3 Something broke or stopped working while you were using it

What were you doing when it broke? What did you do next?

4 You realized something important while doing something else

What were you doing when you realized? What was your reaction?

The Alibi Game üîç

What's an Alibi?

An alibi is proof that you were somewhere else when a crime happened. In this game, you'll need to prove where you were!

üïµÔ∏è The Crime:

Someone stole all the chocolate from the school vending machine yesterday between 4:00 PM and 5:00 PM!

How to Play:

  1. Student creates their alibi - Where were you? What were you doing? Who was with you?
  2. Teacher asks detailed questions - Trying to catch inconsistencies!
  3. Student must remember all details - No contradictions allowed!

Example Questions Teacher Will Ask:

Time & Place:

  • Where exactly were you at 4:15 PM?
  • What room? What building? Inside or outside?
  • How long were you there?

Actions:

  • What were you doing when it was stolen?
  • What were you wearing?
  • What were the people around you doing?

Details:

  • Who were you with? What were they doing?
  • What were you talking about?
  • Did you see anyone suspicious?
  • What happened before 4 PM? What did you do after 5 PM?

Trick Questions:

  • So you were studying... but earlier you said you were playing video games?
  • You said you were alone, but who was Tom then?
  • The library closes at 4 PM on Tuesdays...
