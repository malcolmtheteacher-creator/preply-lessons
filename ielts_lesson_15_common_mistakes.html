Task Response - Common Mistakes

Task Response errors lose you the most marks. Click each card to see the correction.

Question: "To what extent do you agree or disagree?"
Student writes: "This essay will discuss both views."
Task Response
CORRECT: "I completely agree that technology has improved education."
Why this is wrong: The question asks for YOUR opinion, not a discussion of both views. You must take a clear position in the introduction.

Impact: This mistake alone can drop you from Band 7 to Band 6 in Task Response.
Question: "What are the advantages and disadvantages?"
Student writes: Two paragraphs on advantages, no disadvantages discussed.
Task Response
CORRECT: Equal discussion of both advantages AND disadvantages
Why this is wrong: The question explicitly asks for BOTH advantages and disadvantages. You must address both parts equally.

Solution: Write one body paragraph for advantages, one for disadvantages. Give them roughly equal length (both 80-100 words).
Body paragraph: "Firstly, technology is good. It helps people. Many people use it."
Task Response
CORRECT: "Firstly, technology significantly enhances educational outcomes by providing students with instant access to vast information resources. For instance, online learning platforms like Khan Academy allow learners to study at their own pace, which is particularly beneficial for students who need extra time to master difficult concepts."
Why this is wrong: Ideas are under-developed. You need specific details, explanations, and examples.

Band 6 vs Band 7 difference: Band 6 students present ideas but don't develop them fully. Band 7 students extend and support ALL main ideas with specific examples.
Question: "Discuss the causes of pollution and suggest solutions."
Student writes: Three paragraphs about causes, half a paragraph about solutions.
Task Response
CORRECT: Equal coverage - 1-2 paragraphs on causes, 1-2 paragraphs on solutions
Why this is wrong: The question asks for TWO things equally: causes AND solutions. Both parts must be fully developed.

Two-part question rule: Split your essay roughly 50-50 between the two parts. If word count is 280, that's about 140 words per part.
Question: "Some people believe sports are important in schools. Others think they waste time. Discuss both views."
Student writes: Entire essay argues sports are important, ignoring the opposite view.
Task Response
CORRECT: Body 1 discusses why sports are important, Body 2 discusses why some think they waste time
Why this is wrong: "Discuss both views" means you MUST present BOTH perspectives fairly, even if you disagree with one.

Discussion essay rule: Always present both sides neutrally before giving your opinion in the conclusion (if asked).
Key Takeaway: Task Response errors happen when you:
  • Don't answer the exact question asked
  • Miss parts of multi-part questions
  • Don't take a clear position when required
  • Under-develop your main ideas
  • Go off-topic or write irrelevant information

Coherence & Cohesion - Common Mistakes

These mistakes make your essay hard to follow. Click to see corrections.

"First and foremost, moreover, in addition to this, furthermore, technology helps education."
Coherence & Cohesion
CORRECT: "Firstly, technology helps education by providing instant access to information."
Why this is wrong: Too many linking words in one sentence! This is called "over-linking" and examiners HATE it.

Simon's advice: "Band 9 uses cohesion in such a way that it attracts no attention." Simple linking words (and, but, because, firstly) are better than fancy alternatives.

Remember: Overuse of linking words can cap your score at Band 6 for Coherence & Cohesion.
Body paragraph with no topic sentence, just jumping straight into examples.
Coherence & Cohesion
CORRECT: Start with clear topic sentence: "Firstly, online learning offers flexibility that traditional education cannot match."
Why this is wrong: Every body paragraph MUST start with a topic sentence that states the main point.

Topic sentence formula: Signposting word (Firstly/Secondly) + main point in one sentence

Impact: Missing topic sentences = Band 6 maximum for paragraph structure.
"Technology is important. Technology helps students. Technology makes learning easier. Technology is used everywhere."
Coherence & Cohesion
CORRECT: "Technology is important because it helps students learn more effectively. This advancement makes education more accessible, which is why it is used in schools worldwide."
Why this is wrong: Repetition of the same subject (technology) without using reference words or pronouns.

Solution: Use reference words: it, this, these, such + nouns (this tool, these methods)

Cohesion tip: After introducing a noun, use pronouns or reference words to avoid repetition.
Paragraph jumps between: online learning advantages â†' teacher salaries â†' classroom size â†' back to online learning â†' student motivation
Coherence & Cohesion
CORRECT: One paragraph = ONE main idea, fully developed
Why this is wrong: Lack of clear central topic in the paragraph. The paragraph jumps around without focus.

Solution: Each paragraph should discuss ONE main idea only. Related points can be mentioned, but they must support the main idea.

Band 7 requirement: "Clear central topic within each paragraph"
Introduction: "I agree technology helps education."
Body 1: "Technology has disadvantages."
Body 2: "However, technology is beneficial."
Conclusion: "I partially agree."
Coherence & Cohesion
CORRECT: Maintain the SAME position throughout (agree in intro, support in bodies, restate in conclusion)
Why this is wrong: Your position keeps changing. This confuses the reader and shows poor organization.

Consistency rule: If you agree in the introduction, BOTH body paragraphs should support this view. Don't change your mind halfway through.

Note: You can acknowledge counter-arguments, but your main position must stay consistent.
Key Takeaway: Coherence & Cohesion errors happen when you:
  • Use too many linking words (sounds unnatural)
  • Don't use clear topic sentences
  • Repeat words without using reference devices
  • Jump between ideas without clear organization
  • Change your position throughout the essay

Lexical Resource - Common Mistakes

Vocabulary mistakes that cost you marks. Click to see corrections.

"People think" (repeated 5 times in essay)
Lexical Resource
CORRECT: Use variety: "Some argue," "Others believe," "Critics suggest," "Supporters claim," "Many consider"
Why this is wrong: Repetition of simple vocabulary shows limited range.

Band 7 requirement: "Uses less common lexical items with some awareness of style and collocation"

Solution: Keep a list of synonyms for common words: people (individuals, citizens), think (believe, consider, argue), important (significant, crucial, vital)
"make a research" / "do a crime" / "make a decision"
Lexical Resource
CORRECT: "conduct research" / "commit a crime" / "make a decision"
Why this is wrong: Incorrect collocations. Words don't combine naturally.

Collocation matters: You can't just substitute synonyms. Words have natural partnerships.

Common collocation errors:
  • make homework (do homework)
  • do a mistake (make a mistake)
  • say a speech (give a speech)
  • gain experience (NOT earn experience)
"goverment," "enviroment," "diffrent," "finaly"
Lexical Resource
CORRECT: "government," "environment," "different," "finally"
Why this is wrong: Spelling errors, even small ones, reduce your score.

Common spelling mistakes:
  • government (not goverment)
  • environment (not enviroment)
  • accommodation (double c, double m)
  • beginning (double n)
  • occurred (double r)

Impact: More than 3-4 spelling errors = maximum Band 6 for Lexical Resource
"The technology helps the education in the modern the world."
Lexical Resource
CORRECT: "Technology helps education in the modern world." OR "Technology helps with education in the modern world."
Why this is wrong: Overuse/misuse of articles (the, a, an).

Article rules:
  • Technology, education (general concepts) = NO article
  • The technology (specific technology) = use "the"
  • In the world = use "the" (only one world)
  • In modern society = NO article (general)
"In this contemporary epoch of the twenty-first century in today's modern world..."
Lexical Resource
CORRECT: "In today's world," OR "In contemporary society," (pick ONE simple phrase)
Why this is wrong: Memorized phrases that sound unnatural. Examiners spot these immediately.

Warning: Phrases like "in this contemporary epoch," "in this day and age," "in this modern era" are overused by test-takers. Examiners know these are memorized.

Better approach: Use simple, natural language. "Today," "Currently," "In recent years" are perfectly fine.
Key Takeaway: Lexical Resource errors happen when you:
  • Repeat the same words too often
  • Use incorrect collocations
  • Make frequent spelling errors
  • Misuse articles (a, an, the)
  • Use memorized, unnatural phrases

Grammatical Range & Accuracy - Common Mistakes

Grammar mistakes that prevent Band 7+. Click to see corrections.

"Technology help students learn better."
Grammar Range & Accuracy
CORRECT: "Technology helps students learn better."
Why this is wrong: Subject-verb agreement error. "Technology" (singular) needs "helps" (not "help").

Rule: Singular subject = singular verb
Plural subject = plural verb

Impact: This is the most common grammar error. Multiple instances = Band 6 maximum for grammar.
"If students will study hard, they will pass the exam."
Grammar Range & Accuracy
CORRECT: "If students study hard, they will pass the exam."
Why this is wrong: Incorrect conditional structure. First conditional = if + present simple, will + verb

Conditional structures:
  • First conditional: If + present, will + verb
  • Second conditional: If + past, would + verb
  • Third conditional: If + past perfect, would have + past participle
"Yesterday, I go to school. Then I study for exam."
Grammar Range & Accuracy
CORRECT: "Yesterday, I went to school. Then I studied for the exam."
Why this is wrong: Tense inconsistency. "Yesterday" requires past tense.

Tense rules:
  • Time markers (yesterday, last year) â†' past tense
  • General facts â†' present simple
  • IELTS essays usually use present tenses for general arguments
  • Be consistent - don't jump between tenses randomly
"Because technology is useful. Many students use it."
Grammar Range & Accuracy
CORRECT: "Because technology is useful, many students use it." OR "Technology is useful. Therefore, many students use it."
Why this is wrong: Sentence fragment. "Because" creates a dependent clause that cannot stand alone.

Solution 1: Combine with main clause: "Because technology is useful, many students use it."

Solution 2: Use a different connector: "Technology is useful. Therefore, many students use it."
"Students which study hard will succeed."
Grammar Range & Accuracy
CORRECT: "Students who study hard will succeed."
Why this is wrong: Incorrect relative pronoun. Use "who" for people, "which" for things.

Relative pronouns:
  • WHO â†' for people (students who...)
  • WHICH â†' for things (technology which...)
  • THAT â†' for both people and things (the book that...)
  • WHERE â†' for places (the school where...)
  • WHEN â†' for time (the day when...)
All simple sentences: "Technology is good. It helps students. Students like it. They use it every day."
Grammar Range & Accuracy
CORRECT: "Technology is beneficial for students because it provides resources that they can use daily to enhance their learning."
Why this is wrong: No variety in sentence structures. All simple sentences = Band 6 maximum.

Band 7+ requirement: "Uses a variety of complex structures"

Complex structures include:
  • Relative clauses (who, which, that)
  • Conditional sentences (if...)
  • Time clauses (when, while, after)
  • Reason clauses (because, since, as)
Key Takeaway: Grammar errors happen when you make mistakes with:
  • Subject-verb agreement (most common!)
  • Conditional structures
  • Tense consistency
  • Sentence fragments
  • Relative pronouns
  • Lack of complex structures (only simple sentences)

Before & After Essay Comparison

See how correcting mistakes improves band scores. Same ideas, better execution!

Question: "Some people believe that technology has made our lives too complex. To what extent do you agree or disagree?"
BAND 6 VERSION (With Mistakes)

Nowadays, the technology is everywhere. Some people thinks it make life too complex. I will discuss both views.

First and foremost, moreover, in addition to this, technology is helpful. It help people. Many people use smartphones. Smartphones are good. They can communicate.

On the other hand, technology have problems. Because it is difficult to use. Old people cannot use it. Young people uses it too much. They uses social media every day.

In conclusion, technology has advantages and disadvantages. People should thinking about it.

Predicted Band: 6.0
Task Response: 6 (unclear position, under-developed)
Coherence: 6 (poor organization, over-linking)
Vocabulary: 6 (repetitive, article errors)
Grammar: 5 (multiple subject-verb errors)

BAND 7.5 VERSION (Corrected)

Technology has become integral to modern life. While some argue that it has made our lives too complex, I completely disagree with this view, as the benefits of technological advancement far outweigh any perceived complications.

Firstly, technology has simplified many aspects of daily life. Smartphones, for instance, consolidate multiple functions into one device, allowing users to communicate, navigate, and access information instantly. This convenience has actually reduced complexity rather than increased it. Furthermore, modern technology often features intuitive interfaces that make even complex tasks accessible to users of all ages and technical abilities.

Secondly, while some older individuals may initially struggle with new technology, this does not mean technology itself is inherently complex. Rather, it reflects a learning curve that can be overcome with proper education and support. Moreover, the younger generation adapts naturally to technological changes, suggesting that perceived complexity is often a matter of familiarity rather than actual difficulty.

In conclusion, I firmly believe that technology has simplified our lives by providing efficient solutions to everyday challenges. The initial learning phase should not be confused with unnecessary complexity.

Predicted Band: 7.5
Task Response: 8 (clear position, well-developed)
Coherence: 7 (good organization, natural flow)
Vocabulary: 7 (varied, appropriate)
Grammar: 8 (complex structures, accurate)

Key Improvements That Raised the Score:
  • Task Response: Clear position from the start (not "discuss both views")
  • Coherence: Removed over-linking, added clear topic sentences
  • Vocabulary: Fixed articles, used varied vocabulary, correct collocations
  • Grammar: Fixed all subject-verb errors, added complex sentences
  • Development: Each idea fully explained with specific examples
