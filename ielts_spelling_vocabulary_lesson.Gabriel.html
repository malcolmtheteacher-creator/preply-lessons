Mistake Analysis:

Remember: "college" ends with "-ege" (like "knowledge")

Be careful with "sh" vs "ch" sounds

Rule: When adding "-ly" to words ending in "-al", keep both letters

Context matters: "live" = reside, "leave" = depart / cause to remain

Remember: "expense" = cost, "expanse" = wide area

Contraction of "will not" = won't (not wan't)

Two words combined: "may" + "be" = maybe

Context: "threw" = past of throw, "through" = via/by means of

Key Spelling Patterns

Strategy: Don't memorize random words. Learn patterns that apply to hundreds of words!

Pattern 1: Words Ending in -ly

Rule: When adding "-ly" to adjectives ending in "-al", keep both 'l' letters.

  • local → locally
  • final → finally
  • personal → personally
  • traditional → traditionally
  • global → globally

Pattern 2: Double vs Single Consonants

Rule: Many spelling errors come from doubling (or not doubling) consonants.

  • occur → occurring (double 'r')
  • benefit → benefited (single 't')
  • prefer → preferred (double 'r')
  • differ → different (double 'f', single 'r')
  • commit → committed (double 't')

Pattern 3: -ance vs -ence

Trick: There's no perfect rule, but knowing common words helps.

  • importance, attendance, guidance (use -ance)
  • difference, existence, experience (use -ence)
  • independence, confidence, evidence (use -ence)

Pattern 4: IE vs EI

Rule: "I before E except after C" (with exceptions!)

  • IE: believe, achieve, field, brief
  • EI after C: receive, ceiling, deceive, conceive
  • Exceptions: weird, height, their, weight

Pattern 5: Homophones (Words That Sound Alike)

Warning: These cause HUGE problems in IELTS essays!

  • there / their / they're
  • to / too / two
  • your / you're
  • its / it's
  • affect / effect
  • accept / except
Practice Strategy: For each pattern, write 5 example sentences using the words correctly.
