üîç Grammar Discovery: Going to vs Will

üì± Story Time: Read and Discover

Sarah's Saturday Morning:

"I'm going to visit my grandmother today. I called her yesterday and made plans. While I'm there, I think we will have tea together - we always do! Oh, it's raining outside. I will take an umbrella!"

‚ùì Can you spot the difference?

  • Which actions did Sarah plan before?
  • Which actions is she deciding now?
  • Which is a prediction (she's not sure)?

üìä The Rules:

Use Going to Will
Plans & Intentions
(decided before)		 ‚úÖ I'm going to study tonight.
(I decided this morning)		 ‚ùå
Spontaneous Decisions
(decide now)		 ‚ùå ‚úÖ I'll help you!
(I just decided)
Predictions
(no evidence)		 ‚ùå ‚úÖ I think it will rain tomorrow.
Predictions with Evidence
(you can see signs)		 ‚úÖ Look at those clouds! It's going to rain! ‚ùå

üî¢ How to Form:

GOING TO:

Subject + am/is/are + going to + base verb

  • I am going to travel
  • She is going to study
  • They are going to play

WILL:

Subject + will + base verb

  • I will travel
  • She will study
  • They will play

üìù What We Learned Today

üéØ Grammar Summary:

GOING TO - For Plans & Evidence-Based Predictions

Structure: Subject + am/is/are + going to + base verb

Use for:

  • Plans you made before: "I'm going to study medicine."
  • Predictions with evidence: "Look! It's going to rain!"

WILL - For Spontaneous Decisions & Predictions

Structure: Subject + will + base verb

Use for:

  • Spontaneous decisions: "I'll help you with that!"
  • Predictions (general): "I think we will live on Mars one day."
  • Promises: "I'll call you tomorrow!"

üìö Key Vocabulary Review:

intention
A plan or goal you want to achieve
"My intention is to learn three languages."
spontaneous
Done without planning, in the moment
"We made a spontaneous decision to go to the beach."
prediction
Saying what you think will happen in the future
"My prediction is that electric cars will be everywhere."
evidence
Something you can see or know that shows something will happen
"There's evidence it will rain - look at those dark clouds!"
plan ahead
To make plans for the future in advance
"I always plan ahead for my vacations."
make up one's mind
To decide what to do
"I can't make up my mind about which university to attend."
goal
Something you want to achieve in the future
"My goal is to become fluent in English."
probably
Most likely, very possible
"I'll probably study at university next year."
