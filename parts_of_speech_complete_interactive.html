👋 Welcome to Parts of Speech!

What are parts of speech? They're the 8 types of words that build every English sentence. Think of them as the Lego blocks of language!

The 8 Parts of Speech:

1. NOUNS
Person, place, thing, idea
2. PRONOUNS
Replace nouns
3. VERBS
Actions & states
4. ADJECTIVES
Describe nouns
5. ADVERBS
Describe verbs, adjectives
6. PREPOSITIONS
Show relationships
7. CONJUNCTIONS
Connect words/ideas
8. INTERJECTIONS
Express emotion

2️⃣ PRONOUNS - Noun Replacements
Simple rule: Pronouns replace nouns to avoid repetition.

🎯 Why Do We Need Pronouns?

WITHOUT pronouns (sounds terrible!):

"Maria loves Maria's dog. Maria's dog is cute. Maria takes Maria's dog to the park every day."

WITH pronouns (sounds natural!):

"Maria loves her dog. It is cute. She takes it to the park every day."

2. "___ raining today."

3. "___ coming to the party."

💡 Memory Tricks:
• its = possessive (like "his" or "her" - no apostrophe!)
• it's = it is (apostrophe shows missing letters)
• their = possessive (their book)
• they're = they are
• there = place/location

⚠️ Another Big Mistake: "Everyone"

Fix this sentence:

"Everyone are ready for the test."
💡 Rule: Indefinite pronouns (everyone, someone, anyone, nobody) are SINGULAR!
• Everyone IS (not "are")
• Someone HAS (not "have")
• Nobody KNOWS (not "know")

1. Opinion (beautiful, ugly, lovely)
2. Size (big, small, huge)
3. Age (old, young, new)
4. Shape (round, square, flat)
5. Color (red, blue, green)
6. Origin (French, German, Chinese)
7. Material (wooden, metal, plastic)
8. Purpose (sleeping bag, running shoes)

💡 Rules:

• Long adjectives (3+ syllables): use more / most (beautiful → more beautiful → most beautiful)
• Irregular: good → better → best, bad → worse → worst

5️⃣ ADVERBS - How, When, Where
Simple rule: Adverbs describe verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. They answer: How? When? Where? How often?

🎯 Quick Test: Do It Differently!

🗣️ Do these actions in different ways:

1. Say "Hello" loudly (then say "Hello" loudly!)
2. Say "Hello" quietly (whisper it)
3. Say "Hello" happily (smile while saying it)
4. Say "Hello" angrily (frown while saying it)

👆 You just used 4 ADVERBS! They describe HOW you said "hello".

The Big Difference: Adjective vs Adverb

Adjective (describes noun) Adverb (describes verb)
She is a quick learner. She learns quickly.
He is a careful driver. He drives carefully.
That's a good idea. She speaks English well. (NOT "goodly"!)

⚠️ CRITICAL Mistake: Good vs Well

Which is correct?

"He plays tennis ____."

good
well

"That's a ____ idea!"

good
well
💡 Memory Trick:
• GOOD = adjective (describes a noun): "a good book"
• WELL = adverb (describes a verb): "he speaks well"

⚠️ Tricky Adverbs: Hard vs Hardly

These mean COMPLETELY different things!

"He works hard." = He works a lot, with much effort.

"He hardly works." = He almost doesn't work at all!

"I ____ slept last night." (meaning: I got almost no sleep)

hard
hardly
⚠️ More Tricky Pairs:
• late (arrived late) vs lately (= recently: "Lately I've been busy")
• near (come near) vs nearly (= almost: "It's nearly finished")
• high (fly high) vs highly (= very much: "highly intelligent")

⚠️ CRITICAL: Subordinating Conjunctions

These conjunctions create complex sentences:

Time: when, while, before, after, since, until
Cause: because, since, as
Condition: if, unless
Contrast: although, though, whereas

⚠️ The BIG Mistake: "Because" vs "So"

Which is correct?

"I was tired, ___ I went to bed."

so
because

"___ I was tired, I went to bed."

So
Because
💡 Rules:
• Never use "because" and "so" together! ✗ "Because I was tired so I went to bed."
• Choose ONE: "I was tired, so I went to bed." ✓ OR "Because I was tired, I went to bed." ✓
• "Because" cannot start an answer: Q: "Why are you late?" A: "Because I missed the bus." ✗
• Better: "I missed the bus." ✓ OR "It's because I missed the bus." ✓

🎯 Fix These Mistakes

1. "Because I was hungry so I ate pizza." ✗

8️⃣ INTERJECTIONS - Emotion Words
Simple rule: Interjections express sudden emotion. They stand alone and don't connect grammatically to the sentence.

Possible answers: Wow! Ouch! Ew! Amazing! Oh no! Yay! Gross!

⚠️ Punctuation Rules

Strong Emotion (!) Mild Emotion (,)
Wow! That's incredible! Well, I think so.
Ouch! That hurt! Oh, I see.
Help! I'm stuck! Um, I don't know.
💡 Important:
• Interjections are common in spoken English
• Use them sparingly in formal writing
• They don't change form or connect to other words
• Different languages have different interjections - these are English ones!
