What Are Collocations?

Collocations are words that naturally go together in English. Using the right collocations is essential for achieving Band 7-8 in IELTS Writing!

💡 Why Collocations Matter

Band 7 Descriptor: "Uses less common lexical items with some awareness of style and collocation"

Band 8 Descriptor: "Uses a wide range of vocabulary fluently and flexibly to convey precise meanings"

Translation: Knowing which words go together shows linguistic sophistication!

Examples of Collocations

✅ Correct Collocation ❌ Common Error Why It Matters
make a decision do a decision Native speakers always use "make"
take measures do measures "Take" is the natural verb here
raise awareness increase awareness "Raise" is the standard collocation
pose a threat make a threat "Pose" suggests potential danger
address an issue solve an issue More sophisticated than "solve"

⚠️ Important Warning

Do NOT memorize phrases like:

  • ❌ "In today's modern society..."
  • ❌ "Nowadays, more and more..."
  • ❌ "It is crystal clear that..."
  • ❌ "Last but not least..."
  • ❌ "As far as I am concerned..."

Why? Examiners immediately recognize these overused phrases. They signal memorization rather than flexible language use!

  • ✅ Use topic-specific academic collocations
  • ✅ Avoid memorized phrases that examiners recognize
  • ✅ Build sophisticated sentences with strong collocations
  • ✅ Paraphrase effectively using varied vocabulary

✅ Natural vs Unnatural Phrases

Click on each phrase to discover if it's natural or unnatural in academic English!

"tackle a problem"
✅ NATURAL!
This is a strong, academic collocation. "Tackle" suggests actively dealing with a difficult problem.
"do a problem"
❌ UNNATURAL!
Native speakers don't say "do a problem." Use "tackle," "address," or "solve" instead.
"witness significant growth"
✅ NATURAL!
Excellent academic collocation! "Witness" is sophisticated and pairs perfectly with "significant growth."
"see big growth"
❌ TOO SIMPLE!
Not wrong, but too basic for Band 7-8. Use "witness significant/substantial growth" instead.
"hold the view that"
✅ NATURAL!
Perfect for expressing opinions in academic writing. Much better than "have the opinion."
"make an opinion"
❌ UNNATURAL!
We don't "make" opinions. Use "hold the view," "take the position," or "believe."
"play a crucial role"
✅ NATURAL!
Excellent way to show importance! This collocation appears frequently in academic texts.
"do an important role"
❌ UNNATURAL!
Roles "play," they don't "do." Always use "play a role" (crucial/vital/significant).

📝Œ Key Learning Points

  • Collocations don't follow logical rules - you must learn them!
  • Using natural collocations shows linguistic sophistication
  • Simple phrases are often "correct" but too basic for Band 7-8
  • Always check collocations in a dictionary when learning new words

💡 How to Learn Topic Collocations

  1. Create a personal "Collocation Bank" organized by topic
  2. Always learn collocations in full sentences (not isolation)
  3. Read Band 8 model essays and highlight collocations
  4. Use the Oxford Collocations Dictionary
  5. Practice using 3-5 collocations in each paragraph you write

⚠️ Memorized Phrases to AVOID

Examiners immediately recognize these overused phrases. They signal memorization rather than flexible language use!

❌ Never Use These Phrases

Why? These appear in thousands of IELTS essays. Examiners know them by heart!

❌ Memorized Phrase ✅ Better Alternative Why It's Better
In today's modern society... In contemporary society... More academic, less clichéd
Nowadays, more and more... There is a growing trend toward...
Increasingly, ...		 Shows sophistication
It is crystal clear that... Evidence suggests that...
It is widely acknowledged that...		 More academic tone
It goes without saying... It is evident that...
Clearly, ...		 More direct and natural
Last but not least... Finally, ...
Most importantly, ...		 Less clichéd
As far as I am concerned... I firmly believe that...
In my view, ...		 More confident tone
There are two sides to every coin... While some hold the view that..., others take the position that... More sophisticated
At the end of the day... Ultimately, ...
In conclusion, ...		 More formal

📝Œ Key Principles

  • Natural is better than clever: Simple, natural phrases score higher than memorized templates
  • Flexibility shows competence: Using the same idea in different ways demonstrates language range
  • Quality over quantity: 3-5 well-placed collocations are better than forcing 20
  • Context matters: Make sure collocations fit naturally into your argument

✅ What Band 8 Writers Do Instead

They use natural academic language that shows linguistic flexibility:

  • Vary their sentence structures naturally
  • Use collocations that fit the context perfectly
  • Paraphrase the question effectively in the introduction
  • Choose precise vocabulary for their specific argument
  • Never sound like they're following a template
