Natural Idioms & Expressions

ðŸ’¡ How to use this section: Click each idiom card to reveal its meaning and see it in context.

Agreement & Support

"I'm completely on board with that"
Meaning: I totally agree and support this idea
Example: "Remote work forever? Yeah, I'm completely on board with that. My commute was killing me!"
"That hits the nail on the head"
Meaning: That's exactly right / perfectly accurate
Example: "You said people are addicted to their phones? That hits the nail on the head - I can't go 5 minutes without checking mine!"
"You took the words right out of my mouth"
Meaning: I was just about to say exactly that
Example: "The food here is overpriced and underwhelming." - "You took the words right out of my mouth!"

Disagreement & Pushback

"I'd have to push back on that"
Meaning: I disagree and want to challenge that point (polite but firm)
Example: "Everyone should work from the office." - "I'd have to push back on that - some people are way more productive at home."
"That doesn't sit right with me"
Meaning: Something about that makes me uncomfortable / I don't like it
Example: "They fired him over one mistake." - "Yeah, that doesn't sit right with me either. Seems really harsh."
"That's a bit of a stretch"
Meaning: That's an exaggeration / hard to believe
Example: "This app will change your entire life!" - "That's a bit of a stretch, don't you think?"

Being Neutral / Uncertain

"I'm on the fence about that"
Meaning: I haven't decided / I can see both sides
Example: "Are you going to the party?" - "Honestly, I'm on the fence about it. I'm pretty tired but it could be fun."
"It's a mixed bag"
Meaning: There are both good and bad aspects
Example: "How's your new job?" - "It's a mixed bag - great salary but insane hours."
"It depends on how you look at it"
Meaning: There are different perspectives / it's subjective
Example: "Is working weekends always bad?" - "It depends on how you look at it. Some people prefer weekdays off."

Deep Conversation Topics

ðŸ’¡ For the teacher: Choose 2-3 topics and let your student speak for 3-5 minutes on each. Listen for fluency, vocabulary, and natural expression use.
Living Abroad & Cultural Identity

You mentioned planning to live abroad...

  • What's the biggest culture shock you've experienced (or expect to experience)?
  • Do you think living abroad changes who you are as a person? How?
  • What part of your own culture do you think you'd miss most?
  • Have you ever felt "between two cultures" or not quite fitting in anywhere?
  • What's one thing from your culture that you wish other countries would adopt?
ðŸ’¬ Useful expressions for this topic:
"It's a culture shock" / "I felt like a fish out of water" / "It takes time to adjust" / "I'm still getting the hang of it" / "It's a whole different world"
Things You've Changed Your Mind About

We all evolve our opinions over time...

  • What's something you believed strongly 5-10 years ago but now think differently about?
  • What made you change your mind?
  • Was it difficult to admit you were wrong?
  • What belief do you hold now that you think might change in the future?
  • Do you think people should change their minds more often, or stick to their principles?
ðŸ’¬ Useful expressions for this topic:
"I used to think... but now I realize..." / "Looking back, I was naive about..." / "That opened my eyes" / "I've done a complete 180 on that" / "My perspective shifted when..."
Work-Life Balance & Career Priorities

How we work is changing...

  • What does "work-life balance" actually mean to you?
  • Would you take a pay cut to work fewer hours? How much?
  • Do you think the "hustle culture" mentality is toxic or motivating?
  • What's more valuable: a job you love that pays less, or a boring job that pays well?
  • Will our generation ever be able to retire, or is that a fantasy?
ðŸ’¬ Useful expressions for this topic:
"The grind never stops" / "Burning the candle at both ends" / "My job is taking over my life" / "I'm living to work instead of working to live" / "Money isn't everything"
Technology & Human Connection

Are we more connected or more isolated?

  • Do you think social media has made us better or worse at real conversation?
  • When was the last time you had a deep conversation with someone face-to-face?
  • Are long-distance friendships "real" friendships if they're only online?
  • Could you go 24 hours without your phone? What would be hardest?
  • Do you think the next generation will be better or worse at human connection?
ðŸ’¬ Useful expressions for this topic:
"We're glued to our screens" / "Doom scrolling" / "FOMO (fear of missing out)" / "Digital detox" / "Living in a bubble" / "Echo chamber"
Personal Growth & Regrets

Learning from our mistakes...

  • What's a skill you wish you'd learned when you were younger?
  • If you could give advice to your 18-year-old self, what would it be?
  • Do you have any major regrets, or do you believe "everything happens for a reason"?
  • What's something embarrassing that happened to you that you can laugh about now?
  • If you could instantly master one skill right now, what would it be and why?
ðŸ’¬ Useful expressions for this topic:
"If I could turn back time..." / "Hindsight is 20/20" / "Live and learn" / "Better late than never" / "You live and you learn" / "No use crying over spilled milk"

Storytelling Like a Pro

ðŸ’¡ Good storytelling = Vivid details + Emotion + Natural flow
Practice telling these stories aloud with your teacher. Aim for 2-3 minutes each.

Story Prompts - Choose One

The Awkward Moment
Tell me about a time when you felt really embarrassed or awkward in public. What happened? How did you handle it? Can you laugh about it now?
The Turning Point
Describe a moment that changed your perspective on something important. What was life like before? What happened? How are things different now?
The Unexpected Connection
Tell me about meeting someone who became important in your life in an unexpected way. How did you meet? What made them special? Do you still keep in touch?
The Frustrating Experience
Talk about a time when you had to deal with terrible customer service, bureaucracy, or an incompetent person. What went wrong? How did you react? Was it ever resolved?
The Achievement
Share something you're genuinely proud of accomplishing - big or small. Why did you want to do it? What challenges did you face? How did it feel when you succeeded?

Storytelling Vocabulary Toolkit

Moving Through Time

At first / Initially / To begin with / Before I knew it / Eventually / In the end / Looking back

Expressing Emotion

I was mortified (extremely embarrassed) / I was over the moon (very happy) / I was gutted (very disappointed) / It blew my mind (shocked/amazed) / I was livid (very angry) / I was on cloud nine (extremely happy)

Creating Drama & Suspense

Out of nowhere / All of a sudden / To my surprise / Would you believe it / And get this... / Here's the kicker / Plot twist:

Describing Reactions

I couldn't believe my eyes / My jaw dropped / I was speechless / I didn't know whether to laugh or cry / I wanted the ground to swallow me up (embarrassed)

Story Structure Template

Try this structure:

1. Set the scene (30 seconds): "So this was back in 2022, I was living in..."
2. Build up (1 minute): "Everything was going fine until..."
3. The climax (30 seconds): "And then, out of nowhere..."
4. The resolution (30 seconds): "In the end... / Looking back..."
5. Reflection: "What I learned from that is..."

Persuasive Language Arsenal

Strong Claims

"There's no question that..." / "It's clear that..." / "The evidence suggests..." / "You can't deny that..." / "Let's face it..." / "The reality is..." / "At the end of the day..."

Emphasizing Your Point

"What's more..." / "Not only that, but..." / "On top of that..." / "To make matters worse..." / "The key thing to remember is..." / "The bottom line is..."

Countering Arguments

"Sure, some people might say... BUT..." / "I hear that argument, however..." / "Critics claim that... but they're missing the point" / "On the contrary..." / "That's a fair point, but..."

Powerful Conclusions

"So, in conclusion..." / "The takeaway here is..." / "What this all boils down to is..." / "At the end of the day, we need to..." / "The way I see it..."
