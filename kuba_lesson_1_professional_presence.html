‚úçÔ∏è Writing Component: Meeting Follow-Up Emails

Why This Matters

You mentioned wanting to improve your writing, especially emails. Good news: The same principles from speaking apply to writing. These expressions translate directly to professional emails.

Today's focus: Writing clear, confident follow-up emails after meetings.

The Bad Example (What NOT to do)

Subject: Meeting

Hi everyone,

Thanks for the meeting today. I think we discussed some good things. We should probably look at the budget again. Maybe we can talk more next week.

Best,
Kuba

Problems: Vague, no action items, no structure, doesn't capture your expertise

The Good Example (Professional & Clear)

Subject: Action Items: Q4 Budget Planning Meeting - [Date]

Hi team,

Just to build on what we discussed this morning, I wanted to summarize the key decisions and next steps regarding Q4 budget allocation.

From a financial perspective, we need to prioritize the following:

  • Marketing: ‚Ç¨250K increase (approved, conditional on ROI tracking)
  • R&D: Equipment purchase deferred to Q1 2025
  • Sales: 3 new hires approved (not 5 as originally requested)

Let me put this in context: These decisions align with our cash flow projections and maintain our 15% operating margin target.

Action Items:

  • Marketing (Anna): Submit ROI tracking framework by Friday
  • Sales (Tom): Provide final job descriptions by Wednesday
  • R&D (Maria): Prepare Q1 equipment proposal with vendors

If I'm understanding correctly, we'll reconvene on November 30th to finalize Q1 planning. Please confirm if this timeline works for everyone.

Best regards,
Kuba

Why this works: Clear structure, uses the expressions naturally, specific action items, demonstrates expertise
