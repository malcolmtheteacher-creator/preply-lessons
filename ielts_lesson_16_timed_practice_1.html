What You Will Do:

1. Read Question (2 min)

Carefully read and understand the essay question. Identify the essay type and what the question is asking.

2. Plan Essay (8 min)

Brainstorm ideas, create your outline, and organize your arguments. This is crucial!

3. Write Essay (27 min)

Write your complete essay following your plan. Aim for 270-290 words.

4. Review & Edit (3 min)

Check for errors, improve vocabulary, ensure coherence. Make final improvements.

Time Management Strategies

The secret to IELTS success: Good time management is just as important as good English. Many students fail not because of language skills, but because they run out of time.

The Ideal 40-Minute Breakdown

Minutes 0-2: Read & Analyze

  • Read question 3 times
  • Identify essay type
  • Underline key words
  • Understand what is being asked

Minutes 2-10: Plan

  • Decide your position
  • Brainstorm 2-3 main ideas
  • Think of examples
  • Create quick outline

Minutes 10-15: Introduction

  • Hook (1 sentence)
  • Paraphrase question (1-2 sentences)
  • Thesis statement (1 sentence)
  • Total: 3-4 sentences

Minutes 15-25: Body Paragraph 1

  • Topic sentence
  • Explanation (2-3 sentences)
  • Example (1-2 sentences)
  • Link to thesis

Minutes 25-35: Body Paragraph 2

  • Topic sentence
  • Explanation (2-3 sentences)
  • Example (1-2 sentences)
  • Link to thesis

Minutes 35-37: Conclusion

  • Restate position (paraphrased)
  • Summarize main ideas
  • Final thought (optional)
  • Total: 2-3 sentences

Minutes 37-40: Review

  • Check for grammar errors
  • Fix spelling mistakes
  • Improve vocabulary where possible
  • Ensure coherence

Emergency: Running Out of Time?

  • Finish your body paragraph quickly
  • Write a 1-sentence conclusion
  • Incomplete essay = lower score
  • Short conclusion > no conclusion

Common Time Management Mistakes

MISTAKE 1: No Planning

Writing without a plan leads to disorganized essays, going off-topic, and wasting time restructuring.

Solution: Always spend 5-10 minutes planning, even if you feel rushed.

MISTAKE 2: Too Much Time on Introduction

Spending 10+ minutes perfecting your introduction leaves no time for body paragraphs.

Solution: Limit introduction to 5 minutes. Body paragraphs are more important!

MISTAKE 3: Writing Too Much

Writing 400+ words wastes time and increases errors.

Solution: Aim for 270-290 words. Quality over quantity.

MISTAKE 4: No Time to Review

Finishing at exactly 40 minutes means you can't check for errors.

Solution: Aim to finish writing at 37 minutes. Reserve 3 minutes for review.

Practice Strategies

For Your Next Practice:

  • Start with 50 minutes: Give yourself extra time initially
  • Gradually reduce: Next practice: 45 minutes, then 42 minutes, then 40 minutes
  • Track your stages: Note how long each part actually takes you
  • Identify bottlenecks: Which stage takes you longest? Practice that specifically
  • Practice planning separately: Do 10-minute planning exercises for different questions
  • Set mini-timers: Use alarms at 10, 25, and 35 minutes to stay on track

Simon's Time Management Tip:

"I always tell my students: if you have to choose between a perfect introduction and a complete essay, choose the complete essay every time. Examiners need to see your full writing ability, which means they need to read all four parts of your essay."

- IELTS Simon (Former IELTS Examiner)
